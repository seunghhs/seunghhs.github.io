High-Efficiency Material Discovery

HTVS & Machine Learning Integration

Overview

This project focuses on accelerating advanced material discovery through the integration of high-throughput virtual screening (HTVS) combined with molecular simulations, and machine learning (ML). By leveraging computational screening and AI, we can identify optimal Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs) with desired properties more efficiently than traditional experimental approaches.

Research Highlight: Ethane/Ethylene Separation

Design and Screening of Metal–Organic Frameworks for Ethane/Ethylene Separation

Separation of ethane and ethylene is considered to be industrially important for various chemical processes, but their similarities make the process expensive. In this study, we integrated computational screening with machine learning to find optimal metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) with high ethane/ethylene selectivity.

Key Achievement: Using our algorithm, a hypothetical MOF structure with an ideal adsorption solution theory selectivity of 3.6 at 298 K and 1 bar was discovered. Furthermore, structural analysis was implemented, and the full adsorption isotherm of some of the top structures was obtained.

Methodology

Machine Learning Model

The model of ML is based on MOF-NET, the artificial neural network model using the embedding technique similar to Word2Vec. The selectivity data were required to train the ML model, and it can be attained directly through molecular simulation.

Molecular Simulation Approach

In this work, selectivity was approximated in two ways:

  • Henry coefficient calculation - Used for ML and genetic algorithms
  • m-GCMC calculation - For more detailed adsorption behavior analysis

The Henry coefficient of each MOF was calculated using the Widom insertion method via RASPA, enabling high-throughput screening of thousands of MOF structures.
