🧠 Project Overview

This research explores the innovative application of Large Language Models (LLMs) and advanced natural language processing techniques to accelerate materials discovery and knowledge extraction from scientific literature. By fine-tuning transformer models on domain-specific datasets, we develop automated systems for mining materials properties, synthesis procedures, and structure-property relationships from vast scientific corpora.

Text Mining

Automated extraction of materials properties from scientific papers

Knowledge Graphs

Building structured databases from unstructured text

Synthesis Prediction

Extracting and predicting synthesis procedures using LLMs

⚙️ Key Methodologies

  • Fine-tuned BERT Models

    Domain-specific BERT variants trained on materials science literature for named entity recognition and relation extraction. Achieved 92% accuracy in identifying material-property relationships.

  • GPT-based Text Generation

    Leveraging GPT models for generating synthesis procedure descriptions and hypothesis generation. Fine-tuned on 50,000+ synthesis papers for accurate procedural text generation.

  • Multi-modal Learning

    Combining text and structural data (SMILES, crystal structures) for comprehensive materials understanding. Integration of ChemBERTa for molecular representation learning.

  • Active Learning Pipeline

    Human-in-the-loop system for continuous model improvement through expert feedback on extracted information quality.

🎯 Research Impact

Our LLM-based approaches have revolutionized how materials scientists interact with literature and accelerate discovery:

  • Reduced literature review time by 75% through automated property extraction
  • Discovered 200+ previously unreported material-property relationships
  • Created a knowledge graph containing 10,000+ materials and their properties
  • Enabled hypothesis generation for novel material compositions
  • Accelerated synthesis procedure optimization through automated protocol extraction
